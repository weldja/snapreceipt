HMRC requirements

What HMRC requires on a VAT receipt

To reclaim VAT on a business expense, HMRC requires specific information to be present on the receipt. SnapReceipt captures and stores all of it.

✅
Supplier name and address
The business that sold you the goods or services.
✅
Supplier VAT registration number
The GB VAT number of the seller — required on full VAT invoices.
✅
Date of supply
When the transaction took place — determines which VAT period it falls into.
✅
Net amount (exc. VAT)
The price before VAT — this is your allowable expense amount.
✅
VAT rate and VAT amount
The rate applied (usually 20%) and the actual VAT charged.
✅
Gross total (inc. VAT)
What you actually paid — the net plus the VAT.
💡 Simplified receipts: For purchases under £250 (inc. VAT), HMRC accepts a simplified VAT receipt showing just the supplier name, date, description, gross total, and VAT rate — the full breakdown isn't required. SnapReceipt handles both formats.
Questions

Frequently asked

Does SnapReceipt extract VAT from receipts automatically?
Yes. When you scan a receipt, the AI reads the VAT amount, net amount, and gross total directly from the image. You don't need to type anything in — it's captured automatically and stored against the receipt record.
What if the AI gets the VAT amount wrong?
You can edit any field before saving. The AI is accurate for clearly photographed receipts, but if the VAT figure is unclear or the receipt is crumpled, just correct it manually — takes a couple of seconds.
Can I use SnapReceipt if I'm not VAT registered?
Yes. If you're not VAT registered, SnapReceipt still logs the gross amount you paid — which is your allowable expense for Self Assessment. The VAT breakdown is captured but you simply don't claim it back. Works the same either way.
Is SnapReceipt Making Tax Digital compatible?
SnapReceipt is designed with MTD in mind. Your receipts are stored digitally with all required fields, and the CSV export can be passed to your accountant or imported into MTD-compatible software. MTD for Income Tax applies from April 2026 for sole traders earning over £50,000.
How long does SnapReceipt store my receipts?
Indefinitely while your account is active. HMRC requires VAT records to be kept for 6 years — SnapReceipt keeps them safe and accessible for as long as you need them. You can also export everything to CSV at any time.
Do I need to download an app?
No. SnapReceipt runs in your browser at snapreceipt.co.uk — nothing to install from the App Store or Google Play. Works on iPhone, Android, and desktop. Add it to your home screen for quick one-tap access.
